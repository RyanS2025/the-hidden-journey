Product Passport #001

Thin Mint Cookie

6 Countries

Before this cookie reaches your hands, its ingredients have traveled across oceans, through factories owned by Italian and Canadian companies, and finally to one of just two bakeries in the entire United States.

🌴

Palm Oil — Indonesia & Malaysia

RSPO Mass Balance certified. Harvested from palm plantations, processed, and shipped across the Pacific.

Confirmed ✓ ~0.25 kg CO₂
🍫

Cocoa — Ivory Coast & Ghana

Sourced from World Cocoa Foundation members. ~70% of the world's cocoa comes from West Africa.

Confirmed ✓ ~0.20 kg CO₂
🌾

Sugar, Flour & Peppermint — U.S.

Sugar and flour from the Midwest, peppermint oil from the Pacific Northwest. Transported by truck and rail.

Estimated ~ ~0.10 kg CO₂
🏭

Baking — South Dakota or Kentucky

ABC Bakers in North Sioux City, SD (owned by Canadian co. Weston Foods) or Little Brownie Bakers in Louisville, KY (owned by Italian co. Ferrero).

Confirmed ✓ ~0.15 kg CO₂
🚛

From the bakery to regional warehouses to your local troop. This leg varies depending on where you live.

True Story
The Emotional Centerpiece

Olivia Chaffin's Story

Olivia Chaffin was a Girl Scout in Tennessee when she decided to look more closely at the label on her cookie box. What she found changed everything.

The palm oil in her cookies carried a "mixed" certification label — meaning it could come from sustainable sources, or it could come from plantations linked to deforestation and labor abuses.

She didn't look away. She researched. She asked questions. And then she launched a petition that got national attention.

"One Girl Scout asked a question. That question became a movement."
